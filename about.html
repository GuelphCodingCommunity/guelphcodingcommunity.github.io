About Us

We're a group of programmers interested in learning everything we can.

The Guelph Coding Community was created in response to a growing interest in the annual CS Games as an avenue for training students in programming topics that aren't covered in a traditional university environment.

Since it's inception in the spring of 2013 the ambition has grown. We want to recruit as many great programmers as possible and establish Guelph as a great city for coders.

The executive team helps to manage the day to day operations and keep the lights on in the event space. We're all very passionate about what we do and would love to talk to you about your interests. After all, it's the members who build the community.

So invite your geeks, suggest ideas, get involved.

Dan Gillis

Dr. Dan Gillis is an Assistant Professor and Statistician in the School of Computer Science at the University of Guelph. His position is co-sponsored by the Saugeen Ojibway First Nations (comprised of the Chippewas of Nawash Unceded First Nation and the Saugeen First Nation) and the University of Guelph.

Dan's research involves studying spatial temporal statistics, ecological and public health risk assessment, alternative data collection methods, user interface and systems design, and community engaged scholarship.

He is co-founder of the Farm To Fork project, and a member of the Guelph-Wellington Seed Community Food Hub. He is an avid runner and cyclist, a lover of yoga, an adventurer, and suffers from wanderlust. Sometimes he sleeps.
